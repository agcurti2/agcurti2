About Me Back to top ↑

I am a sixth year Mechanical Engineering Ph.D. candidate at the Center for Robotics and Biosystems (CRB) at Northwestern University. I strive to be a technical thought leader investigating solutions to important problems in science and engineering. I am specifically interested in the design and control of resource limited robots, and solving the engineering challenges associated with the development, deployment, and continuous operation of multi-robot and swarm robotic systems. For robots and robotic swarms to be practical, they must operate with limited resouces, for extended durations, and in environments shared with humans. In my research, I am working to solve many of these problems so that humans and robotic swarms can operate seamlessly to accomplish long duration tasks from agricultural monitoring and disaster response to entertainment and collective construction.

This website highlights five of my major projects. The first two, MP2 and MP3, describe the design of two single-rotor flying vehicles modeled after the original Maneuverable Piccolissimo (MP). These projects serve as proof that one can scale down flyers to very inexpensive, minimalistic designs and still achieve controlled flight with onboard sensing and localization. The 2D Shape Formation project explores a collective swarm behavior (forming a shape) while taking into account the limited energy resources onboard each robot and the need for the swarm to react to a human. The final two projects explore similar concepts as 2D Shape Formation, but in 3D.
